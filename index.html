Dr. Kalpana Shankhwar
Assistant Professor, IIIT-Delhi


I am an Assistant Professor at IIIT-Delhi in the department of Human Centered Design. I did my B.E. from Shri G. S. Institute of Technology and Science in Mechanical Engineering, M.Tech from IIT Guwahati in mechanical Engineering with Machine Design specialization and my PhD from National Taiwan University also in Mechanical Engineering with Mechanical Design specialization. I have published my PhD research work in three reputed international SCI journals as a first author. In addition, I have coauthored four international SCI journal articles with international collaborators. My current research interests are extended reality (XR)/(AR, VR, MR), haptic technology, artificial intelligence, digital twin and finite element method (FEM). I worked as an Engineer in an automobile industry, MAN Trucks India Pvt. Ltd. for a year (2012 – 2013). Prior to earning my PhD, I worked as an Assistant Professor at KIIT University in department of mechanical engineering for almost two years (2015 – 2017). My other interests include travelling, listening to music, singing, cooking and playing pool.

Education


Over the span of my academic journey, I have traversed diverse educational landscapes, culminating in a profound expertise in Mechanical Engineering. My academic odyssey commenced with a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Shri G. S. Institute of Technology and Science, Indore, India. The foundation laid during those formative years equipped me with fundamental engineering principles, setting the stage for my subsequent achievements. Eager to delve deeper into the world of mechanical intricacies, I pursued a Master's degree at the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. Here, I specialized in Machine Design, focusing my efforts on a captivating thesis that explored temperature and thermal stress analysis of Ultrashort Pulsed laser welding. This endeavor fortified my grasp of finite element methods and their practical applications in welding processes. My academic voyage reached its zenith with a Ph.D. from the esteemed National Taiwan University. My doctoral research, encapsulated in a groundbreaking thesis, delved into the realm of extended reality-based training and tutorial systems. My work not only enabled hands-on practice of manual metal arc welding but also facilitated the visualization of finite element analysis. This innovative work holds the promise of revolutionizing training paradigms in the realm of mechanical design and analysis. Collectively, my academic pursuits represent a tapestry of theoretical knowledge, hands-on experience, and innovative exploration. As I stand at the confluence of these educational milestones, I am poised to channel my erudition into transformative contributions to the field of Mechanical Engineering.
